Manifesto

Design should
confront, not comfort

Brutalism strips away the gradients, the rounded corners, the subtle shadows — everything that softens the blow. What remains is structure, hierarchy, and honesty.

We believe interfaces should be legible, not decorative. Functional, not fashionable. True to their medium. Raw concrete was never a mistake — it was a statement.

Principles

No decoration.
Only structure.

01

Zero Radius

Every corner is 90°. Rounded corners soften. Square corners declare. We declare.

02

Thick Borders

3–5px structural borders. Not hairlines — declarations. Every boundary is intentional.

03

Single Accent

#DD614C. One color. One purpose. Used for emphasis, never decoration.

04

Monospace Labels

All metadata, labels, and supporting text in Space Mono. Body text in Inter. Two voices, maximum contrast.

05

Hard Shadows

4px offset, 0 blur. Brutalist shadows don't float — they shift. They acknowledge the page plane.

06

Asymmetric Grid

Jarring, unexpected compositions. Comfort is not the goal. Discomfort creates attention.
