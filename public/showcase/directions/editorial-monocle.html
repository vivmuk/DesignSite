The Quiet Revolution in How We Build Homes

Across the hilltops of northern Portugal, a generation of architects is rejecting spectacle in favour of something rarer: buildings that listen to the land. Their materials are local stone, their timelines are unhurried, and their results are redefining what it means to dwell. We spent three months with five practices that are quietly reshaping residential architecture — not through manifestos, but through mortar.
