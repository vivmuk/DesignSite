The Color Wheel

The foundation of color theory—understanding relationships between hues.

360°
Primary: Red, Yellow, Blue — cannot be mixed from other colors
Secondary: Green, Orange, Purple — mixed from primaries
Tertiary: Six colors mixing primary + secondary
Value: Lightness to darkness of a color

Color Harmony

Proven color schemes that create visual balance and appeal.

Complementary

Colors opposite each other on the wheel. High contrast, vibrant look.

Triadic

Three colors evenly spaced. Balanced and colorful without clashing.

Analogous

Colors next to each other. Creates serene, cohesive designs.

Split Complementary

Base color + two adjacent to its complement. Less tension than complementary.

Tetradic (Double Split)

Four colors in rectangular formation. Rich, diverse palettes.

Color Psychology

Understanding the emotional impact and cultural associations of colors.

Red

Energy, urgency, passion. Stimulates appetite—popular in food brands.

Blue

Trust, stability, professionalism. Most universally liked color.

Green

Growth, health, nature. Associated with sustainability and finance.

Yellow

Optimism, creativity, caution. Grabs attention but can cause eye strain.

Purple

Luxury, creativity, wisdom. Historically associated with royalty.

Orange

Enthusiasm, warmth, affordability. Calls to action without urgency.
